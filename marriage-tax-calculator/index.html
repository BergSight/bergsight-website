Will you win or lose in 2032?

Swiss voters approved individual taxation on 8 March 2026. From 2032, every person files their own tax return regardless of marital status. Enter your household incomes to see your estimated federal tax change.

Reform approved · In force 2032 Federal direct tax only · Free tool
ℹ️

Why federal tax only? The new individual tariff is set by federal law and is known. Cantonal and municipal taxes — which make up roughly 70–75% of your total tax bill — will also change, but the cantons have until 2032 to set their new rates. No calculator can accurately model what has not yet been decided. This tool shows your federal tax change, which is a genuine and often significant part of your total bill.

What exactly changes in 2032
Tax returnsInstead of one joint return, each spouse files their own. Income and assets are assessed separately. No more combining incomes for rate progression.
Child deduction doublesThe federal child deduction increases from CHF 6,800 to CHF 12,000 per child, split equally between parents. A significant gain for families.
Pension withdrawals separatePillar 2 and Pillar 3a withdrawals will be taxed per person independently. Staggering withdrawals between spouses in different years becomes more advantageous.
Cantons follow by 2032Cantonal and municipal taxes — roughly 70–75% of your total bill — will also shift to individual assessment, but rates are not yet known. Expect a similar directional effect.
