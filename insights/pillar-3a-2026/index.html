Pillar 3a in 2026: the limit, the tax saving,
and a major new rule

The 2026 contribution limit is unchanged — but 2026 brings the biggest change to Pillar 3a in years. For the first time ever, you can now top up missed contributions from previous years.

By Lukas 6 min read Written by a human

Employees with a pension fund
CHF 7,258
Maximum annual contribution. Same as 2025.
Self-employed without a pension fund
CHF 36,288
Or 20% of net earned income, whichever is lower.

New from 1 January 2026
Retroactive top-up payments are now permitted
For the first time in the history of Pillar 3a, you can now make top-up payments to cover missed contribution years — going back up to ten years. Both the current year contribution and the top-up are fully tax-deductible.
